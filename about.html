Bio

Christina Sheppard Futrell has been a North Carolina Superior Court Certified Mediator since 2012 and a practicing attorney since 2003. Christina has been in private practice serving Greenville, NC and the surrounding areas since 2008. In addition, to assisting parties with mediating their issues, Christina continues to represent clients in a wide range of civil matters that give her a firsthand understanding of the issues that separate parties and the lasting effects when common ground cannot be reached.

Christina is an adjunct professor of Business Law at East Carolina University. Prior to entering private practice, she has worked as both Assistant In-House Counsel and General Counsel to the Republican Party of Florida, where she was involved in all manner of election law issues, general business representation and complex litigation.

Originally from Tallahassee, Florida, Christina is a 1999 graduate of The Florida State University with a B.S. degree in Political Science and Public Relations. After working with the Florida Legislature for a year, Christina returned to law school where she received a J.D. from Regent University in Virginia Beach, VA in 2003.
